Extranet

An extranet extends the intranet by allowing limited access to external users, such as suppliers, business partners, or clients. It creates a controlled environment where outsiders can securely interact with the organization’s internal systems. For example, a supplier might use an extranet to check inventory levels or submit orders directly. Extranets strengthen collaboration beyond the organization while maintaining security through authentication and access controls.
