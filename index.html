Internet Terminologies

Select a category to explore

The study of Internet Terminologies helps us understand the fundamental concepts that make the web function. These terms are grouped into three main categories: Networking, Web Concepts, and Architecture. Each category highlights a different aspect of how computers and the internet communicate, organize, and deliver information.

The Networking category covers protocols and systems such as TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP, and FTP. These are the rules and services that allow computers to exchange data reliably across the internet. The Web Concepts category focuses on the World Wide Web itself, including browsers, servers, HTML, URLs, and the evolution of the web from Web 1.0 to Web 4.0. Finally, the Architecture category explains how systems are structured, including client–server models, multi-tier designs, intranets, and extranets.
